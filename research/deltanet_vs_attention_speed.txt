DeltaNet vs Standard Attention: Training & Inference Speed Research
===================================================================
Date: 2026-04-04
Context: 300M param model, 512 context, single RTX 4080 Super, want fastest training

=== 1. TRAINING SPEED: DeltaNet is SLOWER than standard attention ===

Hard numbers from the Gated DeltaNet paper (Yang et al., ICLR 2025):
Figure 3: Training throughput of 1.3B models on a single H100 GPU

At 2K context (closest to our 512):
  Transformer++ (FlashAttention-2):  ~60 Kt/s   <-- FASTEST
  Samba (hybrid Mamba+attn):         ~57 Kt/s
  Gated DeltaNet-H1 (hybrid):       ~55 Kt/s
  Gated DeltaNet-H2 (hybrid):       ~54 Kt/s
  Mamba2:                            ~53 Kt/s
  Gated DeltaNet (pure):            ~50 Kt/s
  DeltaNet (pure):                   ~50 Kt/s

Key quote from the paper:
  "Transformer++ achieves the best performance in the 2K context window domain,
   thanks to the highly optimized Flash-Attention-2 kernel."

  "The training speed of DeltaNet+ is between that of Mamba and GLA.
   All linear-time models outperform Transformer++ in longer-sequence
   training settings."

  "Although we propose a new hardware-efficient algorithm, the training speed
   still lags behind that of GLA due to overhead caused by modeling
   state-to-state dependencies."

At longer sequences (8K-32K):
  Transformer++ sustains ~50-60 Kt/s (uses sliding window attention)
  Linear models (DeltaNet, Mamba2) maintain ~40-45 Kt/s
  Transformer++ NEVER drops below linear models in the tested range

CRITICAL FINDING: At short sequences (512-2048), Transformer++ with
FlashAttention-2 is ~17-20% FASTER than Gated DeltaNet for training.

Why DeltaNet training is slower:
  1. WY representation overhead: the chunk-parallel form needs extra work
     to marginalize over the d_head dimension (d_head x d_head state matrix)
  2. State-to-state dependencies: unlike GLA's element-wise gating,
     DeltaNet's matrix-valued update rule requires more complex computation
  3. FlashAttention-2 is extremely well-optimized for short sequences
     with minimal memory access overhead
  4. The crossover where linear attention beats quadratic attention is
     somewhere beyond 32K tokens for training throughput

=== 2. INFERENCE SPEED: DeltaNet is MUCH FASTER ===

This is where linear attention dominates:
  - Standard attention: O(n) per new token (must attend to all previous tokens)
  - DeltaNet: O(1) per new token (fixed-size state, no KV cache growth)

Qwen3-Next (80B-A3B with DeltaNet hybrid) concrete numbers:
  - At 128K input tokens: 3x faster than Qwen3-30B-A3B, 10x faster than Qwen3-32B
  - At 32K+ context: >10x higher throughput
  - At 4K context: ~7x faster prefill, ~4x faster decode

Based (linear attention + local attention, 1.3B):
  - Generation: 24x higher throughput than FlashAttention-2
  - Prefill: 56% faster than FlashAttention-2 at 4K context

Memory advantage:
  Qwen3.5-9B (24 DeltaNet + 8 full attn layers):
  - DeltaNet state: 12MB fixed (regardless of context length)
  - Full attention KV cache: grows linearly with context

=== 3. QUALITY: Hybrid 75/25 matches pure attention ===

Qwen3.5 architecture:
  - 3:1 ratio: 75% Gated DeltaNet layers, 25% full attention layers
  - Every 4th block uses standard full attention
  - This hybrid consistently matches or exceeds pure attention quality

Gated DeltaNet 1.3B (trained on 100B tokens):
  - Outperforms Mamba2 and original DeltaNet on perplexity
  - Hybrid variants (DeltaNet + sliding window attn) outperform
    pure Transformer++ on downstream tasks
  - Wiki PPL: GDN 16.42 vs Mamba2 16.56

At Qwen3.5 scale (up to 397B total / 17B active params):
  - MMLU-Pro: 82.5 (Qwen3.5-9B) vs 80.9 (Qwen3-30B pure attn)
  - AIME 2026: 91.3 (Qwen3.5 flagship)
  - Quality matches or beats pure attention across all benchmarks

=== 4. WHAT QWEN 3.5 ACTUALLY CLAIMED ===

Qwen's claims are primarily about INFERENCE efficiency, not training speed:
  - "Efficient Hybrid Architecture: Gated Delta Networks combined with sparse
    MoE deliver high-throughput inference with minimal latency and cost overhead"
  - "Near-100% multimodal training efficiency compared to text-only training"
    (this refers to multimodal overhead, NOT DeltaNet vs attention)

Qwen3-Next (predecessor) training cost claim:
  - Used <10% of Qwen3-32B's training GPU hours
  - BUT this is largely due to MoE (3B active of 80B total), not DeltaNet
  - 90.7% less training cost than Qwen3-32B

Qwen NEVER claimed DeltaNet trains faster than standard attention.
They claimed it INFERS faster with similar quality.

=== 5. VERDICT FOR OUR USE CASE ===

Our setup: ~300M params, 512 context, single RTX 4080 Super, want fastest training

TRAINING SPEED:
  DeltaNet will be ~15-20% SLOWER to train than pure FlashAttention
  at 512 context length. This is because:
  - FlashAttention-2 is extremely optimized for short sequences
  - The WY chunk-parallel representation has overhead for the d_head x d_head state
  - The crossover point where linear attention beats quadratic is far beyond 512
  - At 512 tokens, O(n^2) with n=512 is only 262K operations per head —
    the constant factors of the WY representation dominate

INFERENCE SPEED:
  Not very relevant at 512 context. Standard attention KV cache at 512 tokens
  is tiny. DeltaNet would win at 4K+ contexts but at 512 the difference
  is negligible for our use case.

QUALITY:
  The hybrid 75/25 approach does work. But for a 300M model trained on
  <4B tokens, we're in a regime where model capacity matters more than
  architecture sophistication. The quality difference between pure attention
  and hybrid DeltaNet at this scale is likely within noise.

RECOMMENDATION:
  For fastest possible training at 512 context on a single GPU,
  standard attention with FlashAttention is the right choice.
  DeltaNet's advantages (inference speed, long context) don't apply
  to our use case. The WY overhead makes it strictly slower for training
  at short context lengths.

  If we later want to scale to longer contexts (4K+), the hybrid approach
  becomes more attractive. But at 512, stick with what's fastest: pure attention.
